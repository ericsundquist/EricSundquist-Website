My name is Eric Sundquist and I am a veteran student at University of California Riverside. I am studying psychology and will receive a Bachelor of Arts at the end of spring. I was born in Anaheim, California on March 24, 1992. I grew up in Temecula, California and graduated from Great Oak High School in 2010. Shortly after graduating from high school, I joined the United States Marine Corps and served from 2010 to 2015 as an Infantry Assaultman. This job required a significant amount of determination, skill, and mental toughness. I received awards during my service including the Good Conduct Medal, National Defense Service Medal, and Afghanistan Campaign Medal. After separating from the Corps, I began my college career at Palomar College in San Marcos, California. I received an Associate of Arts in University Studies and transfered to University of California Riverside to attain a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology.

My hobbies and interests include listening to various generes of music, weightlifting, spending time with friends and family, cooking, and hiking. I enjoy traveling and hope to find a career that allows me to continue to do so. I have visited coutries such as Austrailia, Japan, Germany, Singapore, and South Korea. Another interest I have would have to be nutrition and learning about the many affets the food we eat has on our bodies.

I have also worked as an Registered Behavior Technician with children ranging from age 8 to age 17 with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). I learned so much from my supervisors and the children I worked with about ASD and behavior modification. I have also worked as a licensed health, accident, and life insurance agent. I learned a lot about financial planning and many basic ways to be financially smart for my future. I intend to pursue a career related to consumer behavior, financial management, or similar business related career with applications of psychological principles.
